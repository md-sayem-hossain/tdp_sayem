Observation 3 (Day Three)

Date: 09/06/2024 10.00AM - 11.00AM
Vanue: D8.12

Overall Description

The class on cloud computing had a fantastic start! The students were engaged and eager to learn about this exciting topic. The lecturer did an excellent job introducing the course, explaining what cloud computing is and why it's important. They used simple language and examples that made it easy for everyone to understand, even if they were new to the subject. In addition, the students had a class task where they worked together to brainstorm ideas about how cloud computing could be applied in different industries. This activity encouraged collaboration and critical thinking among the students, further enhancing their understanding of the subject. The lecturer also talked about the assignments and how they relate to cloud computing, which helped set clear expectations for the class. Overall, it was an informative and engaging session that left everyone excited to learn more about cloud computing!

Compilation of teaching-learning and classroom management materials

  • Outstanding: The observation displayed a rich variety of teaching materials, including detailed summaries and articles on classroom management. The introduction of cloud computing was seamlessly integrated, and student engagement was consistently high. Questioning techniques were sharp, fostering critical thinking.
  • Mastering: The observation included solid summaries on teaching and management, with a decent grasp of cloud computing. Student engagement was noticeable, though questioning techniques could be more incisive.
  • Developing: While some materials on teaching were present, more depth is needed. The introduction of cloud computing lacked detail. Student engagement varied, and questioning techniques were adequate but could be improved
  • Beginning: The observation lacked sufficient teaching materials and depth. Cloud computing introduction was superficial. Student engagement was low, and questioning techniques need significant improvement.

Delivery of content with purpose in mind

  • Outstanding: The content captivates the audience with its intriguing nature and demonstrates a profound understanding of its purpose. It elucidates concepts clearly, ensuring the e-portfolio's effectiveness.
  • Mastering: The main content is purposefully delivered, elucidating concepts effectively. However, some areas could be clearer or more engaging to enhance overall impact.
